Overview
Musicathon 2026 is a 7-day online hackathon hosted by Musixmatch from June 15 to June 22, 2026. Submissions close on June 22, 2026 at 14:00 CEST / 05:00 PT. Winners will be announced the week of June 30, 2026.

NO PURCHASE IS NECESSARY TO ENTER OR WIN. A PURCHASE DOES NOT INCREASE THE CHANCES OF WINNING.

Eligibility
The Contest is open to individuals who are at least 18 years of age (or the age of majority in their jurisdiction, whichever is higher) at the time of entry. Void where prohibited by applicable law.

The Contest is not open to residents of, or persons located in, the following jurisdictions: Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, Russia, Belarus, and the Ukrainian regions of Crimea, Donetsk, Luhansk, Kherson, and Zaporizhzhia. The Contest is also not open to residents of China, Brazil, or India due to local prize-promotion regulations requiring filings that the Organizer has not undertaken.

Employees of Musixmatch S.p.A. ("Organizer"), Replit, Inc. ("Sponsor"), their respective affiliates, subsidiaries, advertising and promotion agencies, suppliers and their immediate family and/or household members are not eligible to participate or win.

Teams
You may compete solo or in a team of up to four (4) people. All members must be registered on the Hub before the submission deadline and must be listed on the team page at the time of submission. A person can belong to one team only. Team changes after submission are not allowed.

Contest Period & Deadline
The Contest Period runs from June 15, 2026 at 00:00 UTC to June 22, 2026 at 14:00 CEST / 05:00 PT (12:00 UTC). Entries must be submitted on the Hub before the deadline; late submissions cannot be reviewed. The Organizer reserves the right to modify or extend the Contest Period with reasonable notice to registered participants.

Technical Requirements
Every submission MUST integrate and actively use at least one Musixmatch Pro API surface in a meaningful way. Projects that do not use the Musixmatch Pro API will be disqualified before scoring.

Use of partner APIs is encouraged but not required. Open-source dependencies are welcome; full project clones, forks-with-rebrand and AI one-shot copies of existing products are not. Code must be your team's own work.

Submission Requirements
A complete submission includes:
• Title and one-liner
• Project description (what it does, how it uses the Musixmatch Pro API, what makes it interesting)
• Cover image
• Working public demo URL and a demo video (max 5 minutes)
• Public source repository (GitHub or equivalent)
• Team members listed on the Hub team page

Only one submission per team is permitted. You can keep editing your project until the deadline; the version live at 14:00 CEST / 05:00 PT on June 22 is the one judged.

Judging Criteria
Jury scores each submission on Originality (25%), Craft (25%), Use of the Musixmatch Pro API (25%), and Impact (25%). Ties are broken by the head of jury.

Musixmatch Pro API Usage
Registered participants receive a Musixmatch Pro API key with Scale-plan access valid for the Contest Period. This key is for Contest use only and must not be shared, resold, or used outside the Musicathon. The Organizer may revoke API access for any participant in violation of this rule or the Musixmatch Pro API Terms of Service.

Content Usage Restrictions
All content accessed through the Musixmatch Pro API — lyrics, metadata, audio analysis data, and any other proprietary content — is subject to the following restrictions:

No commercial use. Content retrieved via the API may not be used for any commercial purpose. Projects are for demonstration and evaluation only.
No bulk storage. Do not bulk-download, cache, scrape, or otherwise persist API content beyond what is strictly necessary for real-time display within your project.
